## Final Recommendation

### Which factors appear most strongly associated with monthly sales?

Based on the multiple regression analysis, the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales are:

- Footfall – Higher customer footfall is associated with higher monthly sales and has the strongest positive relationship in the model.
- Marketing Spend – Higher marketing spend is associated with higher monthly sales.
- Inventory Availability (%) – Better inventory availability is associated with higher monthly sales.
- Competitor Distance (km) – This variable has a statistically significant negative relationship with monthly sales in the model and may require further business investigation.

These variables have p-values below 0.05, indicating that they are statistically significant predictors of monthly sales.

### Which variables should leadership focus on?

Leadership should focus on:

- Increasing customer footfall through targeted marketing and promotional activities.
- Optimizing marketing spend to improve sales performance.
- Maintaining high inventory availability to reduce lost sales due to stock shortages.
- Investigating the impact of competitor distance on store performance to better understand the observed relationship.

These variables have the strongest and most reliable association with monthly sales in the regression model.

### Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

The variables region_South and store_type_HighStreet should not be over-interpreted because their p-values are greater than 0.05. Based on this analysis, there is not enough evidence to conclude that these variables have a meaningful impact on monthly sales.

Similarly, the simple regression analysis showed that avg_discount_pct is not a statistically significant predictor of monthly sales when analyzed on its own.

### What business action would you recommend?

The retail chain should prioritize actions that increase customer footfall, optimize marketing investments, and maintain high inventory availability, as these factors show the strongest association with monthly sales. Leadership should also investigate why competitor distance shows a negative relationship with sales before making any strategic decisions based on this finding.

### What risks or limitations should leadership keep in mind?

The selected multiple regression model explains 82.07% of the variation in monthly sales, but 17.93% of the variation remains unexplained. In addition, some variables included in the model are not statistically significant and should not be used as the basis for business decisions. The model should be used as a decision-support tool rather than the only source for making strategic decisions.

### Why does regression show association but not automatically prove causation?

Regression analysis identifies relationships between variables, but it does not prove that one variable directly causes changes in another. A variable may be associated with monthly sales because it is influenced by other business conditions that are not included in the model. Therefore, business decisions should combine regression findings with business knowledge and, where possible, additional analysis or controlled experiments before concluding that a factor causes changes in sales.
